About Me

I am a second year Masters student in Computer Science at the University of Minnesota - Twin Cities graduating in Spring 2020. My main interests are computer graphics and game engine programming.

Something that sets me apart is my drive to be the best that I can be. There are many days where I code all day at work, come home, and continue coding on my own projects to learn more and improve. In school I hate the thought of skimming by with easy projects. I am always picking project topics that are new to me and routinely go back to try and make improvements on past projects. My ongoing personal project is a game engine. I am constantly thinking of how new features should be implemented, and redoing old sections as I learn more and more about this field through internships, classes, and personal exploration.

When I am not programming, I enjoy outdoor activities, such as rock climbing and long distance running. As an undergraduate student I competed on the university's rock climbing team and was an officer my last year. Running has always been a part of my life; I started when I was 10 years old and ran competitively through most of my K-12 years. While injuries sometimes slow me down, I always return. Both of these activities have taught me the importance of persistence and practice-- skills that have served me well in and out of sports.

I am currently looking for full time jobs in the areas of graphics or game engine programming starting Summer or Fall 2020. I am also open to positions and related fields and internships if no full time positions are available.
